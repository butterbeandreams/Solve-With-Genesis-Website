# Case Study Intake — internal use, not published

Fill this out right after any new engagement wraps, while it's fresh. When you have
enough of these, add a new `.case` block to `case-studies.html` following the same
structure as the existing five.

Keep every case study honest to this same four-question shape — no exceptions,
no filling in gaps with invented specifics.

---

## Engagement: [client/project name — can be anonymized, e.g. "Series B fintech" or "Regional healthcare group"]

**Date / duration:**

**Case tag** (2-3 words, matches the small caps label on the site, e.g. "Vendor Strategy," "Governance Design"):

### Situation
What was the business problem? Who was affected, and what happened if it went unsolved?
(2-3 sentences, specific, no jargon.)

### Approach
How did you think about it? What was the actual decision or framework applied —
not just "I fixed it," but the reasoning a reader could learn from.
(2-3 sentences.)

### Result
What changed, ideally with a real number (cost, time, risk reduction, adoption).
If there's no clean number, describe the concrete outcome instead of inventing one.
(1-2 sentences.)

### The underlying question
What universal executive problem does this answer, beyond the specific
industry/client? (Matches the italic line under each case on the site.)
One sentence, framed as a question.

---

## Reminders

- Never publish a number you can't stand behind if a prospect asks a follow-up question.
- If a detail is confidential, anonymize the client, not the facts. ("A mid-market
  healthcare company" is fine. Changing the actual dollar figure is not.)
- Five case studies grew from one job. The sixth doesn't need to come from a
  different company to be worth adding — but it's an equally good moment to note
  if it's your first engagement as Solve with Genesis specifically, since that's
  worth its own case study once it exists.
